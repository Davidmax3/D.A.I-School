Introduction:

D'Accurate International School, comprises of nursery, primary and secondary school.
The School was established 'September 2017', and since then has excelled both morally,
physically, educationally and spiritually.

Aim:

At D'Accurate International School, our aim is to provide a nurturing and stimulating
environment where students can develop their full potential. We are committed to
fostering a love for learning, cultivating critical thinking skills, and promoting personal growth.
Through our dedicated faculty, innovative teaching methods, and diverse range of educational
opportunities, we strive to empower our students to become compassionate, lifelong learners
who are prepared to embrace the challenges and opportunities of the future. Together,
we are building a community that values academic excellence, character development,
and the pursuit of dreams. Join us on this journey of exploration, discovery,
and transformation as we inspire minds and shape a brighter future.

Our policy:

Devotion/Assembly:

This is an essential part of the school activities that is compursary to both students and teachers.
At this time, prayers are said, and the National and school anthem are sang.
This also serves as an avenue for important information to be passed across

Security:

Security is most essential for our living, every members or staff is expected to be extra
security-conscious at all times. All students and pupils must sign in and sign out so as
to control their movement.

Fighting/stealing:

These are highly punishable offences, it is expected that any act of agression, assault or bully
should be reported to the school authority.
Stealing can result into punishment or suspension.

Damage of school properties:

If any student or pupil damage any of the school properties, their parents would be
notified and urged to arrage for replacement. This policy is set to curb or reduce
damaging of school properties.

Punctuality:

Every students or pupils is expected to be in the school promises before 7:50am,
Failure to do so would result into punishment.
Teachers are expected to be in the school premises before 7:30.
